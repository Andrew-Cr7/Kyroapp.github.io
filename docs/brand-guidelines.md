# Kyro Brand Guidelines

# Brand Overview

## Brand Name

Kyro

## Tagline

Train Anywhere. Train Kyro.

## Mission

To make fitness accessible anywhere in the world by removing the barriers that prevent people from training while travelling.

## Vision

To become the global platform for flexible gym access.

A user should be able to arrive anywhere in the world, open Kyro, find a gym, purchase access and start training within minutes.

---

# Brand Positioning

Kyro sits at the intersection of:

* Travel
* Fitness
* Freedom

We are not:

* A fitness coaching app
* A workout tracking app
* A nutrition platform
* A gym membership provider

We are a platform that connects people to gyms wherever they are.

---

# Target Audience

Primary Audience:

* Travellers
* Digital nomads
* Business travellers

Secondary Audience:

* Flexible gym users
* Expats
* Fitness enthusiasts exploring new gyms

---

# Brand Personality

Kyro should feel:

## Adventurous

We encourage exploration and movement.

## Reliable

Users should trust Kyro to help them find quality gyms.

## Practical

We solve real-world problems.

## Ambitious

We are building a global product.

## Approachable

We are friendly and easy to understand.

---

# Brand Values

## Freedom

Fitness should not be tied to a single location.

## Consistency

People should be able to maintain their routines anywhere.

## Simplicity

Finding a gym should be effortless.

## Exploration

Travel and fitness should work together.

## Trust

Users and partners should feel confident using Kyro.

---

# Tone of Voice

## General Tone

* Clear
* Human
* Friendly
* Confident
* Practical

We speak like experienced travellers sharing useful advice.

---

## Avoid

* Corporate jargon
* Buzzwords
* Overly formal language
* Aggressive sales language
* Excessive hype

Avoid phrases such as:

* Revolutionary
* Disruptive
* Industry-leading
* Game-changing
* Next-generation

---

## Prefer

Instead of:

"We are revolutionising the fitness industry."

Use:

"We make it easier to find a gym while travelling."

---

# Brand Messaging

## Core Message

Finding a gym while travelling should be simple.

---

## Supporting Messages

Train wherever you are.

Stay consistent while travelling.

Explore more without sacrificing your fitness.

Fitness should travel with you.

Train Anywhere. Train Kyro.

---

# Visual Identity

## Logo

Primary Logo:

Kyro wordmark.

Secondary Logo:

Kyro angle mark ("<" symbol).

The angle mark can be used:

* Profile images
* Favicons
* App icons
* Highlight covers
* Watermarks

---

## Logo Usage

Always provide adequate spacing around the logo.

Never:

* Stretch
* Distort
* Rotate
* Add effects
* Change proportions

Keep logo usage clean and minimal.

---

# Colour Palette

## Primary Colour

Forest Green

Hex:

#314A3D

Purpose:

* Brand recognition
* Headers
* Primary buttons
* Key UI elements

---

## Secondary Colour

Cream

Hex:

#ECE7D8

Purpose:

* Backgrounds
* Cards
* Light sections

---

## Accent Colour

Tan

Hex:

#C8B08E

Purpose:

* Highlights
* Icons
* Accent elements
* Calls to action

---

# Colour Principles

Forest Green should dominate.

Cream should provide balance.

Tan should be used sparingly to draw attention.

Do not overuse accent colours.

---

# Typography

## Primary Font

Outfit

Used for:

* Headings
* Social media
* Website
* Marketing materials

---

## Font Style

Prefer:

* Bold headlines
* Clean layouts
* Strong hierarchy

Avoid:

* Decorative fonts
* Script fonts
* Excessive font combinations

---

# Photography Style

Images should feel:

* Real
* Aspirational
* Active

Preferred subjects:

* Travellers
* Gyms
* Training
* Airports
* Cities
* Remote work lifestyles

---

## Avoid

* Stock-photo style corporate imagery
* Overly posed fitness photos
* Generic office imagery

---

# Social Media Style

Content should feel founder-led.

People should feel like they are following a real journey.

---

## Content Mix

Travel: 40%

Fitness: 40%

Founders / Startup: 20%

---

## Social Media Personality

Educational.

Relatable.

Authentic.

Occasionally humorous.

Never arrogant.

---

# Website Principles

The website should prioritise:

1. Clarity
2. Simplicity
3. Trust
4. Conversion

Every page should clearly answer:

* What is Kyro?
* Who is it for?
* Why should I care?
* What should I do next?

---

# Partner Communication Style

When speaking with gyms:

Be professional.

Be concise.

Focus on value.

Focus on revenue generation.

Focus on customer acquisition.

Avoid technical language whenever possible.

---

# User Communication Style

When speaking with users:

Focus on:

* Convenience
* Flexibility
* Simplicity
* Consistency

Always emphasise the problem being solved.

---

# Brand Do's

* Show real experiences
* Show real gyms
* Show real travel
* Show the founders
* Focus on customer problems
* Use simple language

---

# Brand Don'ts

* Overcomplicate messaging
* Use corporate buzzwords
* Focus excessively on app features
* Create content purely for views
* Copy competitors

---

# Competitive Positioning

Kyro is not competing on price.

Kyro competes on:

* Convenience
* Flexibility
* Discovery
* Global accessibility

The goal is not to be the cheapest option.

The goal is to be the easiest way to train anywhere.

---

# Brand Promise

Wherever life takes you, your training does not need to stop.

Train Anywhere. Train Kyro.
