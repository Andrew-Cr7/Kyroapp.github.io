# Kyro SEO Roadmap

Last updated: 2026-06-18

Owner: Kyro growth

Status: Living document

## Executive Summary

Kyro's SEO moat should be built around one specific territory: flexible gym access while travelling. The goal is not to become a general fitness publisher. The goal is to become the most useful search result for travellers, digital nomads, business travellers and flexible gym users who need a place to train away from their home gym.

This roadmap prioritises pages that can produce waitlist signups now and bookings later. It follows the current business context in `/docs`: Kyro is pre-launch, London is the first launch market, day passes are the first commercial product, and SEO should support waitlist growth, future pass sales and topical authority.

The first 100 pages are structured as a content ecosystem:

1. High-intent launch pages for London and the United Kingdom.
2. Country and city hubs for the recommended expansion markets.
3. Airport, station, business district and neighbourhood pages where search intent is specific and easier to satisfy.
4. Problem-led guides that answer the friction points Kyro exists to solve.
5. Comparison and authority pages that attract links and support commercial pages.

Do not judge this roadmap by search volume alone. Many of the best Kyro pages will have modest volume but strong conversion intent because the searcher is actively trying to access a gym.

## SEO Strategy Overview

### Strategic Positioning

Kyro should own the intersection of:

- Gym access.
- Gym discovery.
- Fitness while travelling.
- Day passes.
- Traveller gym problems.
- Digital nomad and business travel routines.

Kyro should avoid:

- Weight loss.
- Bodybuilding.
- Workout plans.
- Nutrition advice.
- Supplements.
- Generic fitness motivation.

### Page Type Priorities

| Page type | Role | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| City day pass pages | Capture booking-ready and waitlist-ready searches | Highest |
| Country hub pages | Build topical structure and route users to city pages | High |
| Airport, station and district pages | Capture urgent local intent with lower ranking difficulty | High |
| Problem-led guides | Answer the reasons travellers fail to train abroad | High |
| Comparison pages | Capture evaluation-stage users and earn internal links | Medium |
| Authority guides | Build moat, backlinks and topical coverage | Medium |

### Market Prioritisation

| Rank | Market | Why this market is prioritised |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | United Kingdom | London is the launch market, commercial relevance is immediate, Kyro can validate waitlist demand and gym supply fastest, and UK city pages can later support domestic expansion. |
| 2 | Japan | Strong travel demand, clear visitor friction, language barriers, local registration complexity and a natural fit for "can tourists use gyms" searches. |
| 3 | South Korea | High travel interest, Seoul has strong gym culture, language and visitor-policy friction create useful long-tail opportunities. |
| 4 | Thailand | Major digital nomad and traveller market, strong gym culture in Bangkok, Phuket and Chiang Mai, and clear flexible-access demand. |
| 5 | Australia | English-language market, large travelling and backpacker audience, strong city-level gym searches in Sydney and Melbourne. |
| 6 | Portugal | Digital nomad demand is concentrated in Lisbon and Porto, making cluster building efficient. |
| 7 | Spain | Barcelona, Madrid and Valencia are high-fit travel and remote-work markets with strong gym discovery intent. |
| 8 | Singapore | Small market but high business-travel intent, strong purchasing power and excellent fit for business traveller pages. |
| 9 | Germany | Berlin is relevant for digital nomads and travellers, but expansion should follow stronger near-term markets. |
| 10 | United States | Massive opportunity but high competition and broad geography. Prioritise only the highest-intent city and district pages at first. |

### Prioritisation Logic

Pages are prioritised using:

1. Business value.
2. Search intent.
3. Ease of ranking.
4. Internal linking value.
5. Relevance to Kyro users.

High-volume generic travel or fitness content is excluded unless it directly supports gym access while travelling.

Use descriptive, natural anchors:

- "gym day passes in London"
- "gyms near Heathrow"

- "gym access for digital nomads"
- "visitor gym access without a membership"

Avoid repetitive exact-match stuffing. Internal links should feel useful to a traveller trying to solve a real problem.

### Authority Flow Model

1. Country hubs organise the site and help search engines understand the market.
2. City pages convert the highest-intent visitors.
3. District, airport and station pages win easier long-tail searches and feed authority upward.
4. Problem guides answer objections and link laterally into the most relevant location pages.
5. Authority guides attract backlinks and distribute authority to commercial pages.
