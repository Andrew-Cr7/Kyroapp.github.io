# Kyro User Model

## Overview

Kyro exists to help people maintain their fitness routine while away from their home gym.

The core problem is not finding a workout.

The core problem is finding convenient, flexible gym access in unfamiliar locations.

Kyro helps remove the friction that prevents people from training consistently while travelling.

---

# Mission

Enable people to train anywhere in the world without the barriers traditionally associated with gym access.

Train Anywhere. Train Kyro.

---

# Core User Types

## 1. Travellers

### Description

People visiting a city or country for leisure.

Typical Stay:

* 1 day
* 1 week
* 1 month

Common Problems:

* Do not want a membership
* Unsure which gyms allow visitors
* Language barriers
* Difficult registration processes
* Limited local knowledge

Goals:

* Continue training while travelling
* Find a quality gym quickly
* Avoid wasting time researching

Success Outcome:

Find a gym and train within minutes.

---

## 2. Digital Nomads

### Description

People living and working remotely while travelling.

Typical Stay:

* Several weeks
* Several months

Common Problems:

* Repeatedly searching for gyms
* Short-term memberships
* Unclear pricing
* Poor local information

Goals:

* Build a consistent routine
* Train regularly
* Access quality facilities wherever they live

Success Outcome:

Quickly establish a routine in a new city.

---

## 3. Business Travellers

### Description

Professionals travelling for work.

Typical Stay:

* 1 day
* 1 week

Common Problems:

* Limited free time
* Unfamiliar locations
* Hotel gyms often insufficient

Goals:

* Stay healthy while travelling
* Minimise time spent searching
* Access quality facilities near accommodation

Success Outcome:

Convenient training without disrupting work commitments.

---

## 4. Flexible Local Users

### Description

People who want gym flexibility without long-term commitments.

Examples:

* Trying a new gym
* Training while visiting friends
* Occasional gym users
* People considering joining a gym

Goals:

* Flexible access
* No contracts
* Try before committing

Success Outcome:

Simple access to gyms when needed.

---

# Ideal Customer Profile

The highest-value Kyro user is:

* Fitness is an important part of their lifestyle
* Travels regularly
* Has disposable income
* Values convenience
* Prefers experiences over possessions
* Uses technology comfortably
* Is willing to pay to save time

This user is likely to become a repeat customer.

---

# User Pain Points

## Before Kyro

Users often experience:

* Hours spent researching gyms
* Language barriers
* Complicated sign-up processes
* Unexpected fees
* Membership requirements
* Local phone number requirements
* Uncertainty about gym quality
* Difficulty comparing options

Many users skip workouts entirely because the effort outweighs the reward.

---

## Emotional Frustrations

Users often feel:

* Frustrated
* Uncertain
* Unmotivated
* Disrupted

Travelling frequently breaks fitness routines.

The user feels they are losing progress.

---

# User Motivations

Users train because they want to:

* Stay healthy
* Maintain progress
* Improve performance
* Reduce stress
* Preserve routine
* Feel good while travelling

Kyro supports these motivations.

# User Journey

## Stage 1

Planning a trip.

User begins thinking:

"How am I going to train while I'm away?"

---

## Stage 2

Arrives in a new city.

User starts searching:

* Gyms near me
* Day pass gym
* Gym for tourists
* Best gym in city

---

## Stage 3

Encounters friction.

Examples:

* No visitor access
* Local phone number required
* Language issues
* Membership contracts

---

## Stage 4

Finds Kyro.

Kyro presents:

* Available gyms
* Clear pricing
* Simple booking
* Trusted information

---

## Stage 5

Purchases pass.

User successfully trains.

---

## Stage 6

Repeats in future locations.

This is where long-term value is created.

---

# Product Principles

When deciding what to build, prioritise:

1. Reduce friction
2. Save time
3. Increase trust
4. Improve convenience
5. Increase booking conversion

Every feature should improve at least one of these outcomes.

---

# Features That Create Value

Examples:

* Clear pricing
* Instant booking
* Mobile check-in
* Reviews
* Facility information
* Location information
* Pass flexibility

---

# Features That Are Lower Priority

Examples:

* Social features
* Workout tracking
* Coaching
* Nutrition tools
* Community forums

These may be useful but do not solve the primary problem.

---

# Content Preferences

Users are interested in:

* Travel
* Fitness
* Productivity
* Remote work
* Adventure
* Self-improvement

They respond well to:

* Practical advice
