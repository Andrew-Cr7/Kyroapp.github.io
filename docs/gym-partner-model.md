# Kyro Gym Partner Model

## Overview

Kyro partners with gyms to sell flexible gym access to travellers, digital nomads, business travellers and local users seeking flexibility.

Kyro acts as a distribution and customer acquisition platform for gyms.

The goal is to help gyms generate additional revenue from unused capacity while exposing their facilities to new customers.

---

# Partner Value Proposition

Kyro helps gyms:

* Generate additional revenue
* Fill quieter periods
* Reach travellers and digital nomads
* Reach business travellers
* Increase brand visibility
* Convert visitors into long-term members
* Gain access to usage data and insights

Kyro is intended to complement existing memberships rather than replace them.

# Customer Types

## Travellers

Users visiting a city for a short period who want gym access without a contract.

Examples:

* Weekend visitors
* Holiday travellers
* Backpackers

---

## Digital Nomads

Users living in a city temporarily.

Examples:

* One week
* One month
* Three months

---

## Business Travellers

Professionals travelling for work who still want access to quality training facilities.

---

## Flexible Local Users

People who:

* Want to try different gyms
* Need occasional access
* Do not want long-term contracts

# Revenue Model

## Day Passes

Primary launch product.

User purchases a day pass through Kyro.

Revenue is split between:

* Gym Partner
* Kyro

Current commercial model:

* Gym receives approximately 80%
* Kyro receives approximately 20%

This may vary depending on future agreements.

---

## Weekly Passes

Future offering.

Provides additional flexibility for longer stays.

---

## Monthly Passes

Future offering.

Provides a lower-cost option for users staying in a location for an extended period.

# Product Principles

When considering partner-facing features, prioritise features that:

1. Increase partner revenue
2. Increase pass sales
3. Reduce administrative workload
4. Improve customer experience
5. Improve retention

Do not prioritise features simply because they are technically interesting.

---

# Common Partner Concerns

## Existing Membership Cannibalisation

Concern:

"Will this reduce memberships?"

Response:

Kyro primarily targets users who would not otherwise purchase a membership.

---

## Peak Time Capacity

Concern:

"What if we become too busy?"

Response:

Partners may control:

* Availability
* Capacity
* Pass types
* Pricing

---

## Operational Complexity

Concern:

"Will this create more work?"

Response:

Kyro aims to make check-in and pass management as simple as possible.

---

## Existing Contracts

Concern:

"We already work with another platform."

Response:

Maintain relationship and revisit when agreements expire.

Record future follow-up opportunities in CRM.

---

# CRM Pipeline Guidance

Important fields:

* Contact Name
* Gym Name
* Email
* Phone
* Current Status
* Next Action
* Follow-Up Date
* Existing Provider
* Contract End Date
